Top 10 Papers — January 2026

1

Parallel Writing of Magnetic Tunnel Junctions by Electrical Control of Interlayer Magnetic Coupling

M. Yang, L. Zhao, R. Shi et al. npj Spintronics 4, 1 (2026) Jan 9, 2026

Demonstrates a parallel writing architecture for MTJs using unipolar current through voltage-controlled magnetic anisotropy (VCMA). Achieves deterministic switching governed solely by the magnitude of unipolar current in an orange-peel coupling system, enabling scalable multi-bit writing.

Paper MTJ VCMA Magnetic Memory
2

A Widely Tunable Spin-Orbit Torque Device through the Silicon Compatible CMOS Platform

Qi Lu, Bin Peng, Ming Liu et al. Advanced Materials 38(4) (2026) January 2026

Reports a large tunable and fully integrated SOT device on standard silicon CMOS technology. Uses an ultrathin ferroelectric film for voltage-controlled SOT with large tunability and low operating voltage. Demonstrates significant acceleration in write speed and lower current thresholds for MRAM.

Paper SOT CMOS MRAM
3

Strain-Decoupled Magnetism in Flexible Spintronic Sensors

Minsun Oh, Yubin Kim, Minjeong Ha npj Spintronics 4, 3 (2026) Jan 27, 2026

Comprehensive review addressing flexible spintronic sensors for wearables, implantable bioelectronics, and microrobotics. Focuses on the critical challenge of strain-induced inverse magnetostriction disrupting magnetic anisotropy, and reviews strategies for strain-decoupled magnetism.

Paper Flexible Sensors Magnetostriction Wearables
4

Analysis of Magnetic Switching in Magnetically Coupled Dual Free Layers Within MTJs for STT-MRAM

Shujun Ye, Koichi Nishioka Advanced Electronic Materials (2026) Jan 29, 2026

Theoretically investigates magnetically coupled dual free layers in MTJs for STT-MRAM. Identifies four distinct switching phases depending on magnetic coupling energy and write current. Shows optimized coupling can halve the write current while achieving thermal stability above 128 in 30 nm devices.

Paper STT-MRAM MTJ Switching
5

Terahertz Spin Torque Nano-Oscillator Based on a Ferrimagnetic Skyrmion Lattice

L. Shen, Q. Shao, J. Akerman et al. npj Spintronics 4, 5 (2026) January 2026

Proposes a spin torque nano-oscillator based on a ferrimagnetic skyrmion lattice, where weak Magnus forces together with skyrmion-skyrmion interactions allow current-driven skyrmions to oscillate at terahertz frequencies. Relevant to neuromorphic computing applications.

Paper Skyrmions Spin Torque THz
6

The 2026 Skyrmionics Roadmap

Sabri Koraltan et al. (67 co-authors) arXiv preprint Jan 23, 2026

Major community roadmap organized into 33 sections covering magnetic skyrmions and topological spin textures. Includes theory, material systems, skyrmion dynamics, DMI-stabilized structures, SOT-driven skyrmion motion, and device concepts for memory and computing.

Paper Skyrmions DMI Roadmap
7

Domain Wall Skyrmion-Based Magnonic Crystal

Z. Wang, X. Zheng, Z. Li, Z. Zhang, X. Wang Physical Review B 113(1) (2026) Jan 5, 2026

Constructs a magnonic crystal using a chain of domain wall skyrmions to manipulate spin-wave propagation. The skyrmion chain is created by leveraging voltage-controlled Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction (DMI), bridging magnonics and skyrmionics.

Paper DMI Magnonics Domain Walls
8

Anomalous Hall Spin Current Drives Self-Generated Spin-Orbit Torque in a Ferromagnet

E. A. Montoya, X. Pei, I. N. Krivorotov Nature Nanotechnology 20, 353-359 Jan 15, 2025

Landmark discovery of a fundamentally new SOT type: the "anomalous Hall torque," where anomalous Hall spin current in a ferromagnetic conductor generates a giant self-generated SOT large enough to negate magnetic damping and enable neuronal-mimicking nano-oscillators.

Paper SOT Hall Effect Neuromorphic
9

Harnessing Orbital Hall Effect in Spin-Orbit Torque MRAM

R. Gupta, C. Bouard, F. Kammerbauer et al. Nature Communications 16, 130 Jan 2, 2025

Demonstrates ~30% enhancement in damping-like torque efficiency using Ru as an orbital Hall effect layer vs. pure Pt. Achieves ~20% reduction in switching current across 250+ devices and 60% reduction in switching power for SOT-MRAM.

Paper SOT-MRAM Orbital Hall Effect Energy Efficiency
10

Low-Power Three-Dimensional Graphene-Based Flexible Magnetic Sensor

Polymers 18(4), 477 MDPI Jan 26, 2026

Proposes a flexible, low-power 3D magneto-impedance sensor based on FeSiB/PI/graphene microcoil/PI/FeSiB heterostructure. Achieves 31 nT/Hz½ (x-axis) and 36 nT/Hz½ (y-axis) resolution with only 76 µW power consumption for wearable and biomedical applications.

Paper Flexible Sensors Graphene Low Power

China-based TMC demonstrated the world's first 8 Mb SOT-MRAM chip using 110 nm technology. The fully integrated wafer-level chip achieves sub-nanosecond write capability, write energy below 1 pJ/bit, and write-error rates down to 10⁻⁶. A major milestone in SOT-MRAM commercialization.

New Quantum Boundary: Spin Size Determines Kondo Effect Behavior

Phys.org / Osaka Metropolitan UniversityJan 20, 2026

Researchers discovered that the Kondo effect behaves fundamentally differently depending on spin size. For spin-1/2, it suppresses magnetism, but for spin-1 and larger, it stabilizes long-range magnetic order. Establishes a new quantum boundary with implications for spintronics and quantum computing.

NewsKondo EffectQuantum

Researchers developed magnetic nanocomposites with iron oxide nanoparticle cores wrapped in bioactive glass coatings. Under magnetic fields, they destroy tumor cells via magnetic hyperthermia while the bioactive coating bonds to bone and stimulates regeneration. A dual-function approach for cancer treatment.
